2018 Mid-year Population Estimates for Northern Ireland

Summary

Introduction

Population estimates for Northern Ireland at 30 June are produced annually by the Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency. Population estimates are based on the most recent Census of population.

Detailed tables for Northern Ireland and the 11 Local Government Districts are available on the NISRA website, as well as those for Parliamentary Constituencies, Health & Social Care Trustsand Super Output Areas.

Northern Ireland's population continues to increase

On 30 June 2018, Northern Ireland's population was estimated to be 1.88 million people.

Between mid-2017 and mid-2018, the population of Northern Ireland increased by 10,800 people, or 0.6%. Just over half the population were female, with 955,400 females (50.8%) compared to 926,200 males (49.2%).

Over the last 15 years, the annual rate of population change in Northern Ireland has fluctuated between a high of 1.1% in 2007 and a low of 0.3% in 2013.

Over the decade from mid-2008 to mid-2018, the population of Northern Ireland has increased by a total of 102,500 people, with an average year-on-year increase of 0.6%.

Natural change is the main contributor to population growth in Northern Ireland

The two main components of population change are natural change and net migration. The latest increase in the Northern Ireland population was a result of natural growth of 6,700 people (23,100 births minus 16,400 deaths) and an estimated growth 4,100 people due to net migration (23,600 people came to live in Northern Ireland and 19,400 people left).

In the year between June 2017 and June 2018, the overall population growth due to natural change and net migration totalled 10,800 people. As in recent years, natural change was the main contributor to the increase in population.

The population of Northern Ireland is aging

Between June 2008 and June 2018, the median age in Northern Ireland increased from 36.6 years to 38.7. The proportion of the population aged 65 years and over has increased since 2008, while the proportion aged 0 to 15 years has decreased.

It is projected that from mid-2028 onwards, there will be more people aged 65 and over in Northern Ireland than children aged 0 to 15.

The estimated population for the 11 Local Government Districts (LGDs) in mid-2018 ranged from 116,800 people in Fermanagh and Omagh to 341,900 in Belfast.
